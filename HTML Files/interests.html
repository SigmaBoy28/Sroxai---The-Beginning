ROBOTICS

I have a great interest towards ROBOTICS! It all started during my 7th grade when i first saw a YouTube video...
From then, it started... from blinking the first built_in LED in an ESP8266 to frying my current ESP32. I have made many small projects with these.
Like:

1. A miniature automatic gate:
I used just a servo, the ESP8266 and an ultrasonic sensor. I programmed the ESP8266 to turn the servo whenever the ultrasonic sensor detected an object.
It stayed like that for 5 seconds and then closed.
It was very simple, but to my friends and teachers it felt so special and complex!

2. A distance measuring gloves
This was another cool project that i did. I wanted to build a Iron Man repulsor, but that wasn't possible.
So, i decided to make a distance measurement gloves. It used the ultrasonic sensor mounted using 2-sided tape on a custom cardboard gloves that i made.
A 2*16 display, showed the distance. But one day, I took the gloves and wore it a bit rushedly, the cardboard teared and the tape go removed.
With frustration, i removed all the things on that, the sensor, display and the .
That's how my second project got destroyed...

3. A people counter:
This was my last completed project. It basically used 2 IR sensors and ESP32, to track number of people that passed through the gate.
I once presented this in my school's science exhibition. It worked well and my teachers liked it.
This was the last project that i successfully finsihed.

4. The Last project
My last project... I wanted to build a Obstacle avoiding car. I bought the 4WD car kit and started assembling it. Programmed my ESP32 too...
At that time, I didn't have a battery that was capable of running that thing. I used the Power supply from my old CPU. Connected everything..
I pressed 'forward', the weels ran for a split second and stopped. That's when i realized that I've fried my ESP32. It didn't connect to my PC after that...

THE END of my roboics journey for now...
After this i temporarily focused my attention towards some Software stuff...

AI

After trying out ROBOTICS... I started to go in pursuit of building my own AI.
Why? Just for fun... I wanted to build JARVIS, but it isn't possible. Here's how my AI journey went:

1. A complete over-shot bot:
ChatGPT... I was just copy-pasting 90% of code from it. I added features that i haven't used till now!
Features like: file_opener, folder_opener, file_deleter, app_opener, website_opener, addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, exponents, factorials..... A lot of features.
Eventually, my code became like a Spaghetti
To the point where i didn't know what it was even capable of... I rage-quitted there.

2. ROAIS - first online bot
Second time... I named it "ROAIS". I wanted to buold it completely by myself, in fact at the start, i even coded a few basic functions.
But, soon it became copy-pasted bot. But this time, i added Gemini-Api to it. First time, it was fun.
Soon, i found out that it had daily-limits. Also, there was very high latency. I gave up and forgot about it to..

3. Roais - better than before This time, i coded around 50% of it myself. I added custom welcoming messages, weather API, ASCII art and other things.
This time i used HuggingFace API just to find it had more latency then Gemini...
I used it for a few days and it felt slower than manually doing things.
So, that's how i quit once again...

4. SCBS: offline bot It is my present bot that i completely programmed myself. It only does basic things like opening apps, websites, timers and other basic things.
I am proud of this cause i completelycreated it myself without seeing anywhere.
SCBS = "System commander bot - Sroxai"

THE END, I still have one goal for now: Build and train an evolving AI that runs completely local and offline for my PC.
I... don't know when to start and where to start, but i will do it.
